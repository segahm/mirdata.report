Webinars

We record full 45-mins interviews, which we cut into several 'episodes' to make content more catchy on social media channels.

Each data talk episode is composed of:

  • An opening introduction slide about you and your background.
  • Main topic discussion, typically, accompanied by slides.
  • Subtitles: transcription of your speech for general accessibility.

Guidelines

Our Webinars aimed at a broad audience of non-technical decision makers. We aim to explain Deep Tech to them in simple ways.
Here are guidelines to help you simplify and adapt your content.

do
don't
do
Illustrate your talk with keywords and simple visuals.
Make sure you have a clear (preferably white) background during the zoom interview.
Your presentation should have a plan that will make it easier for us to cut your speech into several small ‘episodes’.
Divide your presentation into 4-5 separate sections/topics. It will help us make valuable standalone ‘episodes’, that can be ‘consumed’ without the need to watch the entire video.
Use slides to help your speech.
Use evocative titles and write in English.
Do a test recording of a couple of minutes and review the sound and picture quality before recording the entire presentation.
Use PDF for the final presentation.
don't
Don’t incorporate too detailed images or too dense content that will distract the user from your speech and the subtitles. “Less is more”.
Don’t use a fake background. The background should be neutral and not distracting for the audience.
Don’t make references and jump on ideas to what you discussed previously in the meeting so people that watch your part 3 will still understand if they didn’t see your part 1.
Don’t make a 60 slide or too detailed presentation, 7-10 slides at the most.
Do not use only images without titles
or text body.
Do not wear a t-shirt with a message. Blank color preferred.
Do not incorporate logos of your company in each slide.
Do not add a lot of effect so people can access the pdf without effect (you can duplicate slides for text appearing effect).
